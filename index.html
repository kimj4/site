    • Senior Capstone Project

    E-discovery is the process of finding electronic documents that may be relevant in a court case. This often takes long hours by a legal team, which makes it expensive and prone to error. I am working in a group of six to develop a machine learning system to aid in its automation.

    We are working with the Enron emails dataset, which is the industry standard, and are using TF-IDF along with LSA to turn these documents into a matrix of numbers that represent their relatedness.

    This matrix is then used to train a random forest algorithm that's being implemented from scratch. This system can then take batches of emails, and assign a strength of relevancy. Because of the possibly very high stakes, we run these documents by a trained professional, who will decide if the returned document is actually relevant. This decision is fed back into the system to be used as additional training data.

    This incremental learning is the part of the project that I am specializing in. The random forest is incrementally trained by deleting and recreating some number of trees, and updating the leave nodes of all others.
